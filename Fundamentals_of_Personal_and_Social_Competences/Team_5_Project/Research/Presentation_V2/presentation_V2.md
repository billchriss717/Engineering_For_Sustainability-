# Cultural comparison between Cameroon and Sri Lanka
## 1. \blue{The Concept of time: "Kinship Flexibility" vs "Cosmic Alignment"}

- \red{\textbf{Cameroon(Relationship-Driven Time):}}
Cameroon time is elastic because human connections tend to overide the clock. If a 
cameroonian is  on his way to a meeting and runs into a relative or an old Friend, it is 
culturally unacceptable to cut the conversation short just to be on time.
Time bends for community obligations

- \red{\textbf{Sri Lanka (Astrologically-Driven time):}}
In Sri Lanka, time doesn't bend for people; it bends for the stars. Before starting 
anything major—opening a business, signing a contract, getting married, or even boiling
milk for the New Year—Sri Lankans consult astrologers for the Nekath (auspicious time).
If the cosmos say the perfect time to sign a document is exactly 10:42 AM, everything 
stops until 10:42 AM.

- \red{\textbf{Presentation Punchline:}} In Cameroon, a meeting starts late because you 
ran into an uncle; in Sri Lanka, a meeting starts late because the planets weren't 
aligned yet.
